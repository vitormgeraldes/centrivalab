CentrivaLab™ 50/34

Orthogonal Centrifugal Membrane Cartridge for 50 mL Conical Tubes

The CentrivaLab™ 50/34 cartridge is a centrifugal membrane filtration device designed to operate inside standard laboratory centrifuges equipped with fixed‑angle rotors. By exploiting the Orthogonal‑Centrifugal Membrane Filter (O‑CMF) principle, the cartridge converts rotor angular velocity directly into transmembrane pressure, eliminating the need for external pumps or pressurisation hardware. The 50/34 configuration is optimised for 50 mL conical centrifuge tubes at an effective rotor angle around 34°.

Physics of Operation

The transmembrane pressure generated by the cartridge is governed by centrifugal hydrostatics. For a uniform fluid with density ρ rotated at angular velocity ω, the pressure drop across a flat membrane positioned at radius rm relative to the free surface at radius rs is given by:

Δp = ½ ρ ω² ( rm² − rs² )

In the CentrivaLab cartridge, the membrane offset δm and free surface offset δ0 define the effective radii rm and rs according to the fill volume. This relationship allows users to predict the operating pressure at a given rotor speed, facilitating reproducible membrane screening under well‑defined hydrodynamic conditions.
