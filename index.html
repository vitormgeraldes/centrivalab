High-Throughput Centrifugal Membrane Screening

CentrivaLab is a rotor-matched cartridge platform for rapid screening of nanofiltration (NF) and reverse osmosis (RO) membranes under controlled hydrodynamic conditions, based on Ortho-Centrifugal Membrane Filter (O-CMF) technology. It is designed to support low-volume, high-information membrane evaluation workflows with realistic concentration polarization behavior.

Current Standard Configuration

CentrivaLab 50/34°
Designed for 50 mL fixed-angle rotors at 34°. Suitable for NF/RO screening campaigns and custom development projects.

Additional rotor-matched versions can be developed for other 50 mL and 96–100 mL formats.

Technology

CentrivaLab leverages the Ortho-Centrifugal Membrane Filter (O-CMF) principle, where centrifugal force drives permeation through a flat-sheet membrane positioned for uniform pressure and reproducible hydrodynamics. The concept was introduced in peer-reviewed work and validated for scaled-down membrane screening, with CFD-supported evidence for controlled concentration polarization and rotor-matched performance.

Journal of Membrane Science (2024)
Introduced the O-CMF concept and demonstrated uniform transmembrane pressure, reduced concentration polarization, CFD-supported analysis, and suitability for membrane characterization and screening.
