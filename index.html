High-throughput centrifugal membrane screening for NF and RO workflows.

CentrivaLab™ translates the Orthogonal Centrifugal Membrane Filter (O-CMF) principle into a scalable product platform for membrane evaluation inside standard laboratory centrifuges, without external pumps or pressurisation hardware.

Current lead configuration

CentrivaLab 50/34 is the current reference configuration for 50 mL conical tubes and fixed-angle rotors around 34°, especially JA-20 class 8 × 50 mL rotors, for fast NF/RO screening under defined centrifugal pressure conditions.

  • Current product: CentrivaLab 50/34
  • Tube format: 50 mL
  • Target use: NF / RO membrane screening
  • Infrastructure: Standard centrifuges

Technology

The Orthogonal Centrifugal Membrane Filter (O-CMF) principle converts rotor angular velocity into transmembrane pressure in rotor-matched cartridge geometries. Published experimental validation against a reference NF crossflow unit showed comparable hydraulic permeabilities, permeation fluxes, rejection values and concentration-polarization behaviour across representative screening conditions.

Why the platform matters

  • Designed to reduce external hardware complexity relative to pump-based screening rigs.
  • Validated in low-volume workflows using membrane areas around 1 cm² and sample volumes around 10 mL.
  • Supports rotor-native cartridge design for better alignment with existing centrifuge infrastructure.
  • Provides a stable commercial architecture for current and future product families.

Core product families

Planned bench-top family for 94 mL rotor class geometries around 25–26°, targeting Sigma 4 × 94 mL and 6 × 94 mL fixed-angle rotors.

  • Focused on standard bench centrifuges operating up to 15,000–20,000 rpm
  • Designed for compact NF/RO screening workflows in widely accessible lab infrastructure
  • Commercial architecture reserved for controlled future release

Why not just use crossflow or stirred cells?

CentrivaLab is not a universal replacement for every filtration rig. It is a focused screening platform designed to simplify early membrane evaluation while preserving a technically meaningful relationship with crossflow-style behaviour.
