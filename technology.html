Technology

CentrivaLab is the commercial platform built on the Orthogonal Centrifugal Membrane Filter (O-CMF) principle.

O-CMF principle

The O-CMF was designed to tackle uneven transmembrane pressure and concentration polarization issues found in conventional centrifugal membrane filters and related centrifugal nanofiltration approaches.

Scientific term: Orthogonal Centrifugal Membrane Filter (O-CMF)

Commercial platform: CentrivaLab™

What was demonstrated

Uniform-pressure design

The flat membrane coupon is positioned perpendicular to centrifugal acceleration to support a more uniform pressure field across the active membrane surface.

CFD-supported analysis

CFD simulations showed that concentration polarization is nearly uniform in time-average terms and that the concentrate chamber is homogenized by centrifugal convection.

Versatile pressure envelope

The 2024 study reports pressure coverage suitable for UF, NF and RO-oriented laboratory work, with experimental nanofiltration shown as the validated case.

Crossflow validation

The 2025 validation study assessed O-CMF against a SEPA CF Cell benchmark using two nanofiltration membranes and standard reference solutes including NaCl, MgSO₄, glucose, sucrose and lactose.
