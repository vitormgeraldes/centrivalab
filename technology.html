Technology

Scientific and engineering basis of the CentrivaLab platform.

O-CMF principle

Orthogonal Centrifugal Membrane Filter (O-CMF) technology uses centrifugal hydrostatics to create transmembrane pressure inside rotor-matched cartridge geometries. This provides a compact route to defined membrane-screening conditions within standard centrifuge infrastructure.

Core idea

The cartridge geometry converts rotor angular velocity into an equivalent pressure field across the membrane. The result is a screening workflow that is compact, reproducible and easier to benchmark than many ad hoc centrifugal approaches.

What stays scientific

In papers, reports and academic discussions, the scientific term should remain O-CMF. The brand CentrivaLab is the commercial platform built on that principle.

Guiding rule: technology language explains the principle; product language explains what the customer can evaluate, buy or request.
